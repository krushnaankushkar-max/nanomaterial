Nanomaterials Science

Exploring materials at the scale of $10^{-9}$ meters. Where physics, chemistry, and engineering meet to redefine the future.

What are Nanomaterials?

Nanomaterials describe materials in which at least one dimension is between 1 to 100 nanometers ($10^{-9}$ m).

Surface Area Effect

As particle size decreases, the surface-to-volume ratio increases drastically, making them highly reactive.

Quantum Effects

At the nanoscale, quantum mechanical effects dominate the optical, electrical, and magnetic properties.

Dimensional Classification

0-Dimensional (0D)

Nanoparticles & Quantum Dots

All dimensions are at the nanoscale. Used in medical imaging and LED displays.

1-Dimensional (1D)

Nanowires & Nanotubes

Two dimensions are at the nanoscale, one is elongated. Carbon Nanotubes (CNTs) are key examples.

2-Dimensional (2D)

Nanosheets & Graphene

One dimension is at the nanoscale (atomic thickness). Famous for high electrical conductivity.

Applications in Modern Science

Medicine (Nanomedicine)

Targeted drug delivery, where nanoparticles carry medicine directly to cancer cells, reducing side effects.

Electronics

Nano-transistors and memory storage devices that are faster, smaller, and consume less power.

Environment

Nanofilters for water purification and nanocatalysts to reduce vehicle emissions.
